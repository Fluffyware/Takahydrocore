Marine Geophysical

01 / Geohazard and exploration seismic

2D/3D High Resolution Marine Seismic

THI provides 2D/3D High Resolution Marine Seismic services for geohazard surveys and oil and gas exploration. The work combines high-technology acquisition systems, experienced personnel, efficient offshore operation, and integrated processing and interpretation.

Final processed HR 3D seismic volumes are prepared for high-quality, high-resolution interpretation so project teams can review shallow hazards, subsurface continuity, and exploration targets with greater confidence.

ConfigurationHigh Resolution and Ultra High Resolution seismic configurations are available.

PerformancePenetration may reach 2,000 m depending on geology, with 3D data resolution up to 6.25 m x 6.25 m.

DeliverablesAcquisition, sophisticated processing, analysis, and interpretation aligned to client objectives.

Offshore Geotechnical

02 / Wireline CPT, sampling, coring, and downhole logging

Offshore Geotechnical Survey

Taka Hydrocore owns and operates geotechnical drilling rigs complete with heave compensated systems that can be installed on suitable vessels of opportunity. The rigs use standard 5.5-inch API drill pipe and can be configured for drilling, CPT, undisturbed sampling, coring, and downhole geophysical logging.

Wireline CPTu operations are performed with pore pressure measurement and field control aligned with ASTM D-5778, ISSMGE, British Standard, and ASTM practice. Real-time data acquisition allows the CPT engineer to monitor tip resistance, sleeve friction, pore pressure, inclination, and estimated soil properties during testing.

Downhole sampling is selected around soil condition, using hydraulic piston samplers for very soft to soft cohesive materials, push samplers for medium stiff to very stiff clay, thick-walled samplers for sand and hard clay, and triple core barrel systems for rock recovery. Samples are logged, photographed, protected, sealed, and prepared for laboratory transfer.

Drilling systemHeave compensated offshore drilling with API drill pipe and configurable drill string.

Wireline testingCPTu, BHA deployment, seabed frame reaction, and real-time acquisition workflow.

Sample controlUndisturbed sampling, coring, onboard description, sample protection, and laboratory handover.

Onshore Geotechnical

03 / Drilling, in-situ tests, and sampling

Onshore Geotechnical Survey

Onshore geotechnical drilling is commonly supported by in-situ tests such as Standard Penetration Test, Pressuremeter Test, Field Vane Shear Test, and Cone Penetration Test with or without pore pressure measurement.

THI selects sampling tools according to soil behavior and consistency. Osterberg fixed piston samplers are used for high-quality undisturbed samples in very soft to soft soils, Mazier core barrel systems support soft to hard soils with alternating layers, and triple core barrel systems are used for rock formations.

In-situ testingSPT, pressuremeter, field vane shear, CPT, and CPTu capability.

SamplingDisturbed and undisturbed sampling using tools selected around soil consistency.

CPT rigsDeveloped CPT rigs with driving capacity from 2.5 tons to 20 tons.

Exploratory Drilling

04 / Mining and resource drilling

Exploratory Drilling

THI provides mining exploratory drilling services focused on high-quality coring, suitable equipment selection, and qualified field geologist or supervisor support. Drilling can be performed using conventional or wireline methods in NQ, HQ, or PQ size.

Depth is configured around the project requirement, typically from 20 m to 700 m, and can be extended depending on rig selection and site condition. Sampling standards can be aligned with client requirements, including resource reporting programs such as JORC-oriented workflows.

MethodsConventional and wireline drilling using NQ, HQ, or PQ coring size.

Rig optionsSkid rigs for shallow to deep programs, including capacity down to 1,000 m.

MobilityWheel, tractor-mounted, and crawler-mounted drilling rigs for different site access conditions.

Nearshore Geotechnical

05 / Shallow water drilling and CPT

Nearshore Geotechnical Drilling

To extend exploratory and geotechnical drilling capability over water, THI builds wooden staging or wooden platforms for shallow water environments down to approximately 3 m to 4 m water depth.

For deeper nearshore work, THI has developed modular pontoon systems that allow drilling and geotechnical survey execution down to approximately 25 m water depth. CPTu on pontoon can be conducted using downhole CPT systems, with future development toward jack-up rig applications for deeper and more reliable operation.

Shallow waterWooden staging and platform setup for nearshore drilling in very shallow water.

Pontoon systemModular pontoon support for drilling and geotechnical survey up to approximately 25 m water depth.

CPTu supportDownhole CPT system capability for pontoon-based nearshore investigation.

Hydrogeology

06 / Groundwater monitoring and water supply

Hydrogeology Drilling

Hydrogeology drilling is intended to study groundwater behavior for environmental purposes, including landfill monitoring and pollution detection programs, as well as groundwater supply development.

THI has experience drilling and installing groundwater monitoring wells and water supply wells using PVC or stainless material. Installation diameter commonly ranges from 4-inch to 8-inch pipe, with depths from 20 m to 350 m and flow capacity that may exceed 55 m3/hour depending on aquifer condition.

EnvironmentalGroundwater monitoring wells for landfill monitoring and pollution detection studies.

Water supplyWell installation with suitable casing material, diameter, and depth configuration.

Mining drainageInclined drilling can be applied so the drilled hole intersects the water-bearing layer.

Seabed Geotechnical

08 / Seabed CPT and vibrocore investigation

Seabed Geotechnical Drilling

THI provides seabed geotechnical survey capability through the development and operation of Seabed CPT and Vibrocore systems. This service supports shallow subsurface investigation where direct seabed data and recovered sediment samples are required before design or construction decisions.

The seabed scope can support marine infrastructure planning, route and site assessment, geohazard screening, near-surface soil interpretation, and laboratory follow-up after sample recovery.

Seabed CPTDirect cone penetration testing for near-seabed soil behavior and strength profiling.

VibrocorePhysical sediment recovery for shallow subsurface description and laboratory testing.

ApplicationUseful for pipeline routes, cable routes, marine facilities, and seabed infrastructure planning.
